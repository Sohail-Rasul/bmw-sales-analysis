Analysis of BMW Global Sales

In this project, we use a dataset from Kaggle, which gives us information on

  • sales volume
  • vehicle models
  • segments
  • engine types
  • pricing
  • marketing spend
  • dealership presence
  • fuel prices
  • and macro-economic indicators such as GDP growth and interest rates.

Note: This is a training dataset and does not correspond to the actual figures.

EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis)

From the analysis performed, we have gained the following insights:

  1. The mean prices of Electric, SUV and Sedans are almost equal, with similar sales in all 3.

  2. The mean price of Petrol cars are higher than Electric, Hybrid and Diesel, all 3 of which have almost similar distributions.

  3. The X3, 3 Series and the i4 are the top 3 selling models

  4. Top 5 after marketing revenue makers are:

    1. X3 Electric
    2. X5 Electric
    3. 3 Series Petrol
    4. i4 Hybrid
    5. i4 Petrol

  5. Least 5 after marketing revenue markers are:

    1. iX Electric
    2. iX Petrol
    3. X7 Petrol
    4. X7 Hybrid
    5. i7 Hybrid

  6. Top 5 Countries by Revenue & Units Sold

    1. China (~6.072B)
    2. UK (~5.488B)
    3. France (~5.029B)
    4. India (~4,972B)
    5. Brazil (~4.959B)

  7. Units Sold by Year does not seem to follow a trend. We need more information to learn about the random dips and boosts in sales.

  8. We have observed a positive linear relationship between Units Sold and Number of Dealerships

  9. Highest Average Sale is around the end of the year (Nov, Dec) which makes sense as it is around the vacation as well as bonus time.

# Define X and y
X = df[['price_usd', 'marketing_spend_usd', 'dealership_count',
        'competition_index', 'segment', 'engine_type', 'country']]

y = df['units_sold']

# Encode BEFORE splitting
X_encoded = pd.get_dummies(X, drop_first=True)

# Split
X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    X_encoded, y, test_size=0.2, random_state=42
)

# Train
lm = LinearRegression()
lm.fit(X_train, y_train)

# Predict on TEST only
y_pred = lm.predict(X_test)

# Evaluate
print("R:", r2_score(y_test, y_pred))
print("MAE:", mean_absolute_error(y_test, y_pred))
print("MSE:", mean_squared_error(y_test, y_pred))
print("RMSE:", np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(y_test, y_pred)))
